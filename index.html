What is Epoch Time?

Epoch time, also known as Unix timestamp, is the number of seconds that have elapsed since January 1, 1970, at 00:00:00 UTC. This point in time is known as the Unix Epoch, and it serves as a universal reference point for computer systems.

Current Epoch Time:
(Updates every second)

This standardized way of tracking time is widely used in computing and programming. It's particularly useful because it:

  • Provides a single number to represent a specific moment in time
  • Is independent of time zones and calendars
  • Makes it easy to calculate time differences
  • Is widely supported across different operating systems and programming languages
