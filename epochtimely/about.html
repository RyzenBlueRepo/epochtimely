About Unix Epoch Time Tools

What is Unix Epoch Time?

Unix epoch time, also known as Unix timestamp, is a way to track time as a running total of seconds since January 1st, 1970 at UTC 00:00:00. This moment in time is called the Unix Epoch, and it serves as a universal reference point used by computer systems around the world.

Why Use Unix Epoch Time?

  • Universal Time Reference: Independent of time zones and calendars
  • Simple Calculations: Easy to perform date/time arithmetic
  • Storage Efficiency: Requires less space than formatted date strings
  • Cross-Platform Compatibility: Works consistently across different systems
  • Programming Friendly: Widely supported in programming languages and databases
